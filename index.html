ORBITAL SIMULATOR ⓘ

Controls Guide

  • Orbit Preset: Select a predefined satellite matching known datasets.
  • TLE Line 1 & 2: Core mechanics standard strings. Tip: Paste a full 2-line or 3-line block directly into Line 1 to auto-parse and fill both.
  • Sample Catalog Numbers: ISS — 25544, Hubble — 20580, Landsat-8 — 39084
  • Swath Width (km): Physical lateral width of the sweeping sensor footprint.
  • Along-Track: Pitch offset (degrees) aiming the sensor forward/backward along the flight path.
  • Cross-Track: Roll offset (degrees) aiming the sensor sideways perpendicular to the flight path.
  • Trailing Rotations: Number of orbital loops to retain in history.
  • Clear Trail: Instantly wipe cached sensor footprints.
  • Altitude Multiplier: Visual exaggeration scale for 3D visibility (does not alter physics).
  • Time Speed: Multiplier accelerating simulation physical time.
  • Show Borders: Toggles geographical vector boundary rendering.

TLE Format Guide ⓘ

A Two-Line Element (TLE) set strictly defines an orbit using 2 lines of mathematically compressed text metrics. You can paste complete data payloads directly into TLE Line 1 to auto-populate both identical datasets.

  • Line 1: Satellite Catalog Number, Classification, Epoch Time, and Atmospheric Drag variables.
  • Line 2: Real-time Keplerian Elements (Inclination, RAAN, Eccentricity, Argument of Perigee, Mean Anomaly, and Mean Motion / orbits per day).

TLE Field Breakdown ⓘ

Line 1 — Identification & Drag

  • Col 3-7: Satellite Catalog Number (NORAD ID)
  • Col 8: Classification (U = Unclassified)
  • Col 10-17: International Designator (launch year + number + piece)
  • Col 19-32: Epoch — Year (2 digits) + Day of Year with fractional time
  • Col 34-43: 1st Derivative of Mean Motion (drag rate, rev/day²)
  • Col 45-52: 2nd Derivative of Mean Motion (usually 0)
  • Col 54-61: BSTAR — Solar radiation / atmospheric drag coefficient
  • Col 63: Ephemeris Type (usually 0)
  • Col 65-68: Element Set Number
  • Col 69: Checksum (modulo 10 hash)

Line 2 — Keplerian Orbital Elements

  • Col 9-16: Inclination (deg) — Tilt of orbit vs. equator
  • Col 18-25: RAAN (deg) — Where plarne crosses equator northward
  • Col 27-33: Eccentricity (implied 0.) — Orbit shape (0 = circle)
  • Col 35-42: Arg of Perigee (deg) — Angle to lowest altitude point
  • Col 44-51: Mean Anomaly (deg) — Current position along orbit
  • Col 53-63: Mean Motion — Orbits per day (speed indicator)
  • Col 64-68: Revolution Number at Epoch
  • Col 69: Checksum (modulo 10 hash)
